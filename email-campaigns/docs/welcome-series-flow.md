# Welcome Email Series

This document describes the Welcome Email Series for new leads.

The purpose of this series is to introduce Dakdan, build trust, and guide the
lead toward booking a consultation.

## MVP Scope (Important)
For the MVP:
- Only the **first 2–3 emails** may be implemented
- The full 5-email series can be added later as a follow-up phase

## When does the series start?
- When a new lead signs up
- The lead must be allowed to receive emails

## Email Schedule

1. **Day 0 – Welcome Email (MVP)**
   - Thank the lead for their interest
   - Brief introduction to Dakdan

2. **Day 2 – Case Study Email (MVP)**
   - Share a short, relevant case study
   - Match the lead’s industry when possible

3. **Day 5 – Educational Email (Optional for MVP)**
   - Explain the idea of *implied sponsorship*  
   - This means brands gain visibility by appearing naturally alongside
     relevant content, without direct ads

4. **Day 10 – Consultation Invite (Later Phase)**
   - Invite the lead to book a consultation
   - One clear call to action

5. **Day 20 – Final Follow-up (Later Phase)**
   - Friendly check-in
   - Ask if the lead is still interested

## Personalization
Emails may include:
- First name
- Company name
- Industry
- Calculator result or lead score (if available)

## When should emails stop?
The series must stop immediately if:
- The user unsubscribes
- The user books a call
- The lead is marked inactive
- The lead is manually suppressed

## Tracking (MVP)
For MVP:
- Track metrics at the **campaign level** (sent, opened, clicked)

Later:
- Add per-email tracking and detailed events

## Compliance (Required)
Every email must:
- Include a CAN-SPAM compliant footer
- Display a physical business address
- Include an unsubscribe link
- Honor unsubscribes immediately
